Example 5. Calculated pH

Data for the chemical system H+, Ca2+ and CO32− was extracted using DATABASE. Then it was modified in SPANA to exchange components for reactions, in this case CO32− was exchanged for CO2 and Ca2+ for CaCO3(cr).

In the diagram the calculated pH is plotted against the amount of added CO2 (from 10−4 to 0.1M) in a closed system that contains an excess (0.05M) of calcite (CaCO3).

Important: the total concentration of H+ must be given, instead of pH, if you wish to plot the calculated pH.

The reaction is: CaCO3(cr) + CO2 + H2O = Ca2+ + 2 HCO3−; but when more CO2 is added, and the pH decreases, most of the added CO2 remains as un-dissociated carbonic acid:
