SED & PREDOM / Activity Coefficient Models

The simplified Helgeson-Kirkham-Flowers (HKF) model

The equation to calculate the activity coefficient, γi, of an ion “i” with electric charge Zi is:
    log γi = −Aγ Zi2 (√I  /  (1 + åBγ √I )) − log(1+0.018 ∑mk) + bγ,MX I

where I is the ionic strength, Aγ and Bγ are Debye-Hückel parameters which are temperature- and pressure dependent (see Table), å is the effective ionic radius (discussed below), and bγ,MX is a solute-specific parameter. The sum (∑mk) is made over all solute species “k” in the aqeuous solution, both charged and neutral. The model is further described in Helgeson, Kirkham and Flowers (1981) (eqs. 121, 165-167, 297, 298), Oelkers and Helgeson (1990) (eqs. 22 & 23) and in Oelkers and Helgeson (1991) (eqs. 3 & 9).

In SPANA (and SED/PREDOM) the HKF model is simplified by using the NaCl parameters:

  • the parameter “å” is the effective ionic distance for NaCl (å = (1.91+1.81) Å = 3.72 Å; see Table 9 in Shock et al. 1992 and Table 3 in Helgeson et al. 1981). This results in åBγ = 1.22 (kg/mol)0.5 at 25°C. The effective ionic distance changes at high temperatures or pressures as described in Shock et al. (1992) (eqs. 4-5, 24-26, Table 3).
  • bγ,MX for NaCl solutions is used, see Table. bγ,NaCl= 0.064 at 25°C.
  • the calculated ionic strength (I) is used instead of ∑mk. This avoids problems when making diagrams where the redox potential, Eh, is outside the stability range of H2O. Convergence problems in the calculations occur if large amounts of either O2(aq) or H2(aq) are calculated when Eh is too high or too low, respectively, because then ∑mk becomes unrealistically large.

The activity of water (H2O) is given by
    log aH2O = − Φ ∑mk / (ln(10) 55.508)

where “Φ” is the osmotic coefficient, calculated using the HKF model as follows:
    Φ = ln(1+0.018 ∑mk)/(0.018 ∑mk) − (2 AΦ (1/∑mk) I3/2 σ(åBγ√I)) + (1/∑mk) (1/2) ln(10) bγ,NaCl I

where AΦ = (1/3) ln(10) Aγ, and
    σ(x) = (3/x3) ((1+x) − 1/(1+x) − 2 ln(1+x))

In SPANA (and SED/PREDOM) these equations are simplified by using the ionic strength value (I) instead of ∑mk. This avoids the convergence problems explained above in diagrams where the redox potential, Eh, is outside the stability range of H2O.

Note that in the calculations the values of log γi are cut-off to be in the range ±(20 Zi2), and log aH2O is cut-off to be in the range ±4.
