Table of Debye-Hückel parameters

The following table lists values for the parameters in the Debye-Hückel model, Aγ, AΦ and Bγ, at selected temperatures and pressures. These values have been calculated from the density and dielectric constant of water, which are also listed in the table.

The density of fluid water, “ρ”, has been calculated using the model by Wagner and Pruss (2002). The dielectric constant of water (relative permittivity, denoted here as “εr”) has been calculated using the equations from Fernández et al. (1997) (at temperatures below 600°C) and from Johnson and Norton (1990) and Johnson et al. (1992) (at temperatures above 600°C).

For convenience, the table lists also the values of åBγ obtained with å = 4.5685 Å, corresponding to åBγ = 1.5 (mol/kg)−½ at 25°C. These values presupose that å does not change with either temperature or pressure.

The table also lists the parameter bγ,NaCl for the simplified HKF model (see equations and Table A-2 in Oelkers and Helgeson (1990).

The pressures between 100 and 350°C correspond to the liquid-vapour equilibrium for H2O. For temperatures < 100°C the reference pressure of 1 bar is used. Below 0°C and above the critical point of water the table lists values at 2 kbar.

Other tables of Debye-Hückel parameters may be found for example in Bradley and Pitzer (1979) (Table II), Helgeson et al. (1981) (Table 1, p.1294-1295), Beyer and Staples (1986) (Table II), Archer and Wang (1990) (Table 5), and Fernández et al. (1997) (Table 17).
